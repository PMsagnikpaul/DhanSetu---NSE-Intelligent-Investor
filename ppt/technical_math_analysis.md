# Technical & Mathematical Analysis
## NSE Intelligent Investor Platform

The NSE Intelligent Investor platform is built upon a foundation of classical quantitative finance mathematics, modernized through the application of deep learning algorithms. This document outlines the core mathematical concepts and formulas utilized across the platform's engine.

---

## 1. Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM) Metrics
The platform compares algorithmic portfolios against standard indices (Nifty 50) using CAPM linear regression to prove outperformance.

### Alpha ($\alpha$)
Alpha represents the "excess return" generated by the AI optimizer that cannot be explained by general market movements. It is the value added strictly by the algorithm's intelligence.
* **Formula:** $\alpha = R_p - [R_f + \beta \times (R_m - R_f)]$
* *Where:* $R_p$ is portfolio return, $R_f$ is the risk-free rate (10-yr G-Sec Yield), $\beta$ is the portfolio beta, and $R_m$ is the market return.
* **Interpretation:** An alpha of `+2.0%` means the algorithmic portfolio outperformed its mathematically expected return by 2%, effectively beating the market.

### Beta ($\beta$)
Beta measures the systematic risk, or volatility, of the portfolio relative to the broader market. It is calculated as the covariance of the portfolio returns with the market returns, divided by the variance of the market returns.
* **Formula:** $\beta = \frac{Cov(R_p, R_m)}{Var(R_m)}$
* **Interpretation:** 
  * $\beta = 1.0$: Portfolio moves perfectly synchronously with the Nifty 50.
  * $\beta < 1.0$: Portfolio is "defensive" and less volatile (e.g., drops less during a crash).
  * $\beta > 1.0$: Portfolio is "aggressive" and amplifies market movements.

## 2. Risk Management & Drawdown Metrics
The **Risk Engine** (`risk_engine.py`) calculates institutional-grade risk metrics to ensure capital preservation.

### Sharpe Ratio
Measures the return of an investment compared to its risk (volatility). 
* **Formula:** $Sharpe = \frac{R_p - R_f}{\sigma_p}$
* *Where:* $\sigma_p$ is the annualized standard deviation of the portfolio's daily returns.
* **Usage:** The Portfolio Optimizer specifically targets maximizing this ratio during Bull regimes.

### Sortino Ratio
A modification of the Sharpe ratio that penalizes only downside volatility (returns below a target or risk-free rate), rather than penalizing all volatility equally.
* **Formula:** $Sortino = \frac{R_p - R_f}{\sigma_d}$
* *Where:* $\sigma_d$ is the standard deviation of negative asset returns.

### Conditional Value at Risk (CVaR / Expected Shortfall)
While standard VaR tells you the maximum loss at a certain confidence level (e.g., 95%), **CVaR** tells you the *expected average loss* if that 95% threshold is breached. It is the ultimate measure of tail risk.
* **Calculation:** It is the mathematical mean of the worst 5% of daily returns in the historical window, annualized: $CVaR_{95\%} = \frac{1}{1-\alpha} \int_{0}^{1-\alpha} VaR_{\gamma} d\gamma$
* **Usage:** During a "Bear" regime, the MPT optimizer switches its objective function from "Maximize Sharpe" to "Minimize CVaR".

### Maximum Drawdown (MDD)
The maximum observed loss from a historical peak to a trough before a new peak is attained.
* **Formula:** $MDD = \frac{Trough Value - Peak Value}{Peak Value}$

## 3. Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT) & Convex Optimization
The **Portfolio Optimizer** relies on Markowitz's Mean-Variance Optimization, supercharged with LSTM predictions.

### The Objective Function
The optimizer uses the SciPy `SLSQP` (Sequential Least SQuares Programming) solver to find a vector of portfolio weights ($w$) that maximizes the Sharpe Ratio:
* **Maximize:** $\frac{w^T \mu - R_f}{\sqrt{w^T \Sigma w}}$
* *Where:* 
  * $w$: Vector of weights for each stock.
  * $\mu$: Vector of **LSTM-predicted** expected returns (this is the novel factor; traditional MPT uses historical mean returns, which are backward-looking).
  * $\Sigma$: The covariance matrix of historical asset returns.

### Constraints & Concentration
* **Bounds:** $0.005 \le w_i \le 0.30$ (Ensures no stock is less than 0.5% or more than 30% of the portfolio).
* **Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI):** Used to measure portfolio concentration. $HHI = \sum_{i=1}^{n} w_i^2$. The platform calculates "Effective N" as $\frac{1}{HHI}$ to show the true diversification level.

## 4. Machine Learning Mathematics

### LSTM Anomaly Detection (Module 1)
To filter out noise from bulk deals and insider trades, an LSTM Autoencoder compresses a 60-day sequence of market data into a latent space and attempts to reconstruct it.
* **Anomaly Score:** Calculated via **Mean Squared Error (MSE)**. $MSE = \frac{1}{n} \sum (x_i - \hat{x}_i)^2$
* *Interpretation:* A high MSE means the current market sequence is highly unusual (the neural network cannot easily reconstruct it). This statistical anomaly acts as a multiplier to signal a high-conviction opportunity.

### Hidden Markov Models (Module 4 - QuantVault)
Financial markets are not stationary; they switch between hidden macro states. The system models this using a **Gaussian HMM**.
* The model assumes an unobservable sequence of latent states $Z_t \in \{Bear, Sideways, Bull\}$.
* **Transition Matrix ($A$):** A $3 \times 3$ probability matrix where $A_{ij} = P(Z_t = j | Z_{t-1} = i)$, representing the mathematical likelihood of the market moving, for example, from Sideways to Bull.
* **Optimization:** The model learns the transition probabilities and the means/variances of the emissions (returns, volatility, yield) using the **Baum-Welch algorithm** (an Expectation-Maximization technique).

## 5. Black Swan "Circuit Breaker" Logic
The mathematical overrides for extreme events:
* **Rolling Volatility Override:** Computes the 10-day rolling standard deviation of index returns. If $\sigma_{10d} \ge 2.5\%$, the circuit breaker trips.
* **VIX Proxies:** India VIX levels above $35.0$ mathematically force the HMM probability vector to collapse into a $100\%$ Bear classification, disabling standard MPT optimization.
